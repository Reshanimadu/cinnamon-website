Ceylon Cinnamon

GI Cinnamon

Ceylon cinnamon can be easily set apart from the other varieties based on its even skin, Golden Brown skin colour, thin bark, low levels of coumarin which is a harmful substance, and sweeter but harsh taste. The cinnamon quills-making technique is a traditional method inherent to Sri Lankans which with their expertise is peeled into unformed thickness skins and the gaps are filled with small cinnamon layers in a cylindrical shape. The unique characteristics of the Ceylon cinnamon are due to the climatic, soil-related, and human-related factors of Sri Lanka.

One might think that GI is only about making the connection between a product and a place but thatâ€™s not it. The entire purpose of the GI is to give value to the skill and the achievement of the human factor who is behind the product as well. Likewise, Ceylon cinnamon endorses the old tradition and the long history which gives life to it making it an internationally celebrated brand.

During a manufacturing process, a variety of societal issues can arise such as environmental, health, and labor related and GI-certified producers should always commit to alleviating these problems by adopting suitable policies. Furthermore, linking a product to its origin indicates that the natural resources should be preserved for the continuation of the product manufacturing. This pushes the producers of GI-certified producers to introduce environmentally friendly and sustainable manufacturing methods to the production process.

GI Cinnamon

Ceylon cinnamon is one of the iconic productions of Sri Lanka and it received the very first European Union Geographical Indication (GI certification) and status on 02nd February 2022. It is now included in the Register of Protected Designations of Origin and Protected Geographical Indications (PGI).

The quality, reputation, and product attributes are usually strongly connected with its geographical origin. The GI logo showcases the connection between the relevant geographical region and the product name. The GI logo acts as an intellectual property right (IP) that protects the product from being misused by third parties.

Cinnamon cut quills, cinnamon powder, cinnamon leaf oil, and bark oil are the four product types of Ceylon cinnamon that are covered by the GI certificate. The whole country is entitled to use the GI logo and it show case the effort in unison of that particular geographical area such as of the cinnamon suppliers, planters, manufacturers, traders, exporters, and other authoritative bodies as well.
