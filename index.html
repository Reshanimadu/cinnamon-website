Ceylon Cinnamon Process



The harvesting is done twice per year and harvesting immediately after a rainy season makes it easier to peel the bark. Only the stems which are 1.2cm-5cm are used for the harvesting.

01 Harvesting

The Cinnamon bark peeling is a labor-intensive process that is usually done by hand by peeling experts. Due to this, about 60% of the production cost is coming from the cinnamon processing. The cinnamon quality is determined by how skillfully the bark is removed.

02 Processing

A rasp knife is used to harvest the soft outer bark. Then a brass rod is rubbed against the stripped stems to loosen the inner bark. The reason for using the brass rod is to avoid the discoloration of the bark during the rubbing process. Then stem cuts are done at 30cm intervals using a stainless steel or brass pointed knife. After that, long cuts are done across the stems to ease off the bark sections and these are known as quills.

03 Processing Stages

These compound quills are put on racks made out of coir and placed in a shady place to prevent bending out of shape. After a 04-05 days period of time, these quills are rolled on a flat and hard surface to tighten the inner content and kept in low sunlight to dry more. However, the rainy or humid season requires mechanical dryers, which are electrical, gas, or biomass.

04 Drying

The grading of the cinnamon is based on several factors such as the bark thickness, appearance (broken or intact), aroma, and flavor.

05 Grading

This is a method of value addition and the ground cinnamon is likely to spoil since the flavor and aroma compounds are volatile.

06 Grinding

The quills are cut into the desired length and packed and sealed in moisture-proof polypropylene bags. The product label should contain all the required information.

07 Packaging

It is important to store the dried cinnamon quills in a moisture-proof packaging and where direct sunlight is not reached. Regular inspection of the quills should be done to identify any deterioration or moisture. The storage area should be hygienic, dry, cool, and without pests.
