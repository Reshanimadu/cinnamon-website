Ceylon Cinnamon Vs Cassia Cinnamon

Cinnamon has a well-defined flavor which changes based on the origin of the cinnamon plant. The most commonly known cinnamon type is Cassia cinnamon and the other one is Ceylon cinnamon.

Even though both of the cinnamon types are filled with antioxidants and have numerous health benefits, there are many differences of these two types.

Cassia cinnamon and Ceylon cinnamon are varieties of cinnamon Cassia is harvested from Cinnamomum cassia and Ceylon cinnamon is harvested from Cinnamomum verum. Ceylon cinnamon is the most preferred cinnamon type of the two. It is more expensive than the Cassia cinnamon due to the production process is more labor intensive and less common than the Cassia.

Both cinnamon types are made out of the cinnamon bark but Cassia cinnamon is harvested when the tree is 20 years old and the 1/16” layer is stripped of its coarse grey skin and kept for drying. During the drying process, the skin bends and the cinnamon stick is made.

How to Identify Ceylon Cinnamon?

Ceylon cinnamon production is more labor intensive and the selected branches are submerged in water to soften the bark outer layer. Then the peelers peel off this layer to get the finer inner bark and this bark is then peeled off in very thin layers and then dried. The drying process makes these layers twisted and after the completion of drying, these layers are put inside each other to make a compound cylindrical shaped quill.

Ceylon cinnamon quill has a thin paper multi-layered form and Cassia cinnamon has an uneven thick texture and a swirl form the colour of Ceylon cinnamon is tan Brown and Cassia cinnamon’s is Reddish dark Brown.

The strength of Ceylon cinnamon is fragile and therefore easily broken when compared with Cassia cinnamon which is tougher

Ceylon cinnamon has a finer flavor because it contains less essential oil cinnamaldehyde compound hence more suited for preparing confectionary items.
